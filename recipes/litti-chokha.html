Litti Chokha Recipe

Description

Litti Chokha is a very traditional and rustic dish of spiced sattu
(roasted black chickpea flour) stuffed whole wheat dough balls called
Litti. This is classically paired with a simple veggie mash essentially
with onion, garlic, green chilies and mustard oil, called the chokha.
It is a delicacy in the food culture of both Bihar as well as Jharkhand.

Ingredients

  • For the dough

    • Wheat Flour - 440 g (2 cups)
    • Ajwain (Carom Seeds) - 1/2 tsp
    • Ghee - 2 tbsp
    • Curd - 3/4 cup
    • Baking Soda - 1/2 tsp
    • Salt - 3/4 tsp

  • For the stuffing

    • Sattu (Roasted Gram Flour)- 200 g (1 cup)
    • Ginger - An inch long piece
    • Green Chilly - 2 to 4 pieces
    • Dhaniya (Green Coriander) - 1/2 cup (finely Chopped)
    • Jeera (Cumin Seeds) - 1 tsp
    • Ajwain (Carom Seeds) - 1 tsp
    • Mustard Oil - 1 tsp
    • Pickle Spices - 1 tbsp
    • Lemon Juice - Of 1 Lemon
    • Salt - according to taste

  • For the Chokha

    • Brinjal - 400 g (1 or 2)
    • Tomatoes - 250 g (4 medium sized)
    • Green chillies - 2 to 4 (finely chopped)
    • Ginger - 1.5 inch long piece (finely chopped)
    • Dhaniya (Green Coriander) - 2 tbsp (finely chopped)
    • Mustard Oil - 1 to 2 tsp
    • Salt - according to taste

Steps

  • Preparation of dough for litti

    1. Filter the flour and keep it in a utensil.
    2. Add Ghee, Ajwain, baking soda and salt in flour, and mix well.
    3. Whisk the curd and mix it in the flour.
    4. Knead a soft dough using warm water.
    5. Cover the dough and keep aside for 30 minutes.
    6. The dough is ready.

  • Preparation of the stuffing for the Litti

    1. Wash, peel and finely chop the ginger.
    2. Wash and finely chop the green chillies after removing stem.
    3. Wash, clean and finely chop dhaniya (coriander) leaves.
    4. Add Sattu, Jeera, Ajwain, green chillies, coriander, lemon
      juice, salt, mustard oil and picle spices in a bowl.
    5. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly.
    6. If the stuffing (Pitthi) seems dry, add 1-2 spoons of water.
    7. The Sattu stuffing (Pitthi) for the Litti is ready.

  • Making the Litti

    1. Break medium sized pieces dough.
    2. Press the pieces of the dough with your fingers to expand
      to 2-3 inches in diameter.
    3. Place 1 to 1.5 tsp Sattu stuffing (Pitthi) on it, wrap up
      the dough piece and close from all sides.
    4. Press this stuffed piece to flatten it a bit
    5. Litti is ready for frying.
    6. Heat the Tandoor(furnace), place the stuffed pieces of dough
      in the Tandoor.
    7. Cook the Litti by turning sides regularly till they turn brown.

  • Making Chokha for Litti

    1. Cook the brinjal and tomatoes on open flame after washing.
    2. Allow them to cool and peel their skins.
    3. Put them in a bowl and mash them with a sppon.
    4. Put the chopped spices, salt, oil and mix properly.
    5. The Brinjal Chokha is ready.

  • Serving the Litti Chokha

    1. Dip hot Litti in melted Ghee to coat it completely.
    2. Litti can also be broken from centre for coating in Ghee.
    3. Serve the Litti with Chokha and some freshly cut onions.
    4. Enjoy the delicious, flavorful Litti and Chokha.
